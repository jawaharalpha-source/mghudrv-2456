How to choose a fund
1

Match the fund to the goal's horizon

Under 3 years: liquid/debt funds. 3–5 years: aggressive hybrid. 5+ years: equity — a broad index fund (Nifty 50 / Nifty 500) is the sensible default.

2

Costs compound against you

Prefer direct plans over regular, and low expense ratios. An index fund at ~0.2% beats most active funds at ~1.5–2% over long periods.

3

Judge 3–5 year consistency, not last month

Compare a fund's rolling returns to its benchmark and category. A fund that never tops the charts but never falls apart usually wins the decade.

A straight word on stock tips

This app will not tell you "the best stock for tomorrow" — because nothing can. Day-to-day moves are close to random, and anyone selling next-day picks is selling noise. What actually works:

•

For days & weeks — don't play

Short-term trading against professionals and algorithms is a losing game for individuals after costs and taxes.

•

For months — momentum, cautiously

If you must, momentum factor funds (e.g. Nifty 200 Momentum 30 index funds) systematise "what's been winning" without single-stock risk.

•

For years — own the market

A broad index SIP has beaten the large majority of active stock-picking over 10-year periods in India. Boring wins.

In a liquid fund or sweep-in FD. Not equity — this money's job is to exist, not to grow.

40

Keep EMIs under 40% of take-home

Lenders' ceiling, and a good personal one. Under ~36% is comfortable.

Pure term insurance, not endowment/ULIP. Cheap at your age, ruinously expensive to delay.
